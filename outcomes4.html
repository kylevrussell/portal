Outcomes

Outcomes is a term with many meanings. With the context of healthcareare the results or consequences of healthcare decisions and interventions. They can be measured in various ways, including mortality rates, quality of life improvements, and patient satisfaction. However, from the persepective of user experience research, outcomes at their core are personally defined as an individual's desired outcomes and can be further broken down into tangible outcomes and intangible aspects. Tangible outcomes are emperical; they can be observed and measured. Intangible outcomes are more subjective and can include feelings of hope, confidence, and empowerment. Both tangible and intangible outcomes are important to consider when evaluating the impact of healthcare decisions and interventions. The flavor of outcomes in focus in this blog are the tangible and intangible desired outcomes, primarily of patients, but also of clinicians.
